Kids face a number of challenges when battling serious health issues, but the Children\u2019s Health Foundation is looking to \u201cChangeIt.\u201d<\/p>\n

In collaboration with Ascend Fundraising Solutions, the new rewards network platform allows users to give back to those in need.<\/p>\n

By signing their credit cards up for the online program, users are able to round up each purchase to the nearest dollar, turning spare change into positive change.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe couldn\u2019t do what we do in making kids feel comfortable and making them feel like it\u2019s (the hospital) maybe not such a scary place without donors in our community,\u201d said Jennifer Baxter, director of community relations and the Children\u2019s Miracle Network.<\/p>\n

She added that the funds donated will go towards programs and services at both TVCC, formerly Thames Valley Children\u2019s Centre, and the Children\u2019s Hospital.<\/p>\n

\u201cThings like our therapeutic clown, child-life specialists, art therapists, Kids in the Block opportunities at TVCC, as well as the Opportunities to Participate program,\u201d Baxter said. \u201cIt\u2019s things like that (which) are not covered by government funding \u2013 they are covered by donors like our community.\u201d<\/p>\n

Kim Woodbridge is an account manager with Ascend Fundraising Solutions and highlights that the rewards program is a win-win for everyone.<\/p>\n

\u201cI know everyone knows of the incredible impact that Children\u2019s Health Foundation has in our community. So, this is a really easy way to be a part of that and give back,\u201d she said. \u201cAt the same time, by becoming a part of the ChangeIt program, you have access to some exclusive benefits, rewards and cashback offers on the purchases that you\u2019re making at both local and national businesses.\u201d<\/p>\n

Users also receive a tax receipt for their donations to \u201cacknowledge and encourage their generosity,\u201d according to the foundation.<\/p>\n

The Children\u2019s Health Foundation and Children\u2019s Hospital see roughly 66,000 families every year, with the TVCC seeing around 11,000 kids annually both across western Ontario, from Windsor to London, Kitchener-Waterloo, Thunder Bay, and remote communities.<\/p>\n

Woodbridge said that the program kicks off at a free launch event at Storybook Gardens Friday evening.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019re encouraging families to come down and have some playtime in the park and learn and even sign up for the ChangeIt program,\u201d she said. \u201cWe\u2019re also going to have the children\u2019s band, The Thinking Caps there, the waterpark is going to be open, the concessions are open, and it\u2019s all just surrounding the program which is so important.\u201d<\/p>\n
